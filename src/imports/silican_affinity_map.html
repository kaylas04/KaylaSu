Clarity of information

4
"I wasn't sure what this company does at first glance — it looked super technical." first impression
Had to search externally to understand what they were selling. comprehension
Homepage needs a single clear sentence that explains the business. value prop
Initially mistook the site for an electronics company, not a materials supplier. positioning

Product understanding

5
A comparison chart would help — e.g. which silicon variant is for batteries vs. other uses. differentiation
Nano-Si vs. Micro-Si distinctions are unclear; products all look the same. naming
Product pages are too short — still unclear what makes each product special. depth
No downloadable datasheet or technical PDF to share with engineering teams. collateral
Listing target industries per product would immediately add clarity. use cases

Trust & technical proof

7
"The site feels a bit like a student project — not very professional." credibility
No clients or case studies mentioned, making it hard to trust. social proof
Would like to see certifications, test results, or ISO links. certification
Published research or third-party validation would increase credibility. validation
No team or founder info — unclear who's behind the company. transparency
Wants confirmation that products are already used in commercial batteries. adoption
No safety standards or third-party testing mentioned — causes hesitation. safety

Business utility

6
"As a purchaser, I care most about order size, lead time, supply capacity — none of that is mentioned." procurement
Unclear whether orders are limited to Taiwan or available globally. geography
No MOQ or logistics info — essential for B2B buyers. logistics
A simple "suitable for industrial-scale use" line would help a lot. scale signal
Delivery timelines matter greatly for manufacturers but aren't shown. lead time
Unclear whether custom formulations are offered vs. fixed specs only. customization

Visual design

4
Looks outdated — feels like a website from the early 2010s. perception
Font is too thin, especially hard to read on mobile. legibility
Product list was difficult to locate. navigation
A more polished UI with clearer hierarchy would make it feel premium. polish

Contact flow

8
Wanted to request a sample but couldn't figure out how. samples
Contact form feels generic — more like a support inbox than a sales channel. intent mismatch
Needs a visible "Get a Quote" button or direct sales path. CTA
Submitted the form but received no confirmation — uncertain if message was received. feedback
No indication of expected response time after submitting. expectations
Would prefer a phone number or live chat for more direct communication. channel
Form doesn't disclose the recipient or whether data is secure. trust
Overall experience feels one-sided — no real invitation to partner. tone
